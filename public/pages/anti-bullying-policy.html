Anti-Bullying Policy

What is Bullying?

  • Bullying, which includes cyberbullying, occurs when a person or group of people repeatedly hurt, embarrass, or frighten another person. Cyberbullying means bullying through the use of technology or any electronic communication regardless of whether the bullying occurs on or off school grounds. Examples of cyberbullying include but are not limited to: text/instant messaging, e-mails, creation of a webpage impersonating another person and/or other electronic means through the internet.

Report bullying communication or situations.

When cyber bulling happens, it is important to document and report the behavior so it can be addressed.

We encourage users to proactively report any bullying, harassment, or abuse that they see or experience on Involvvely

Steps to Take Immediately

  • Don’t respond to and don’t forward cyberbullying messages.

  • Keep evidence of cyberbullying. Record the dates, times, and descriptions of instances when cyberbullying has occurred. Save and print screenshots, emails, and text messages. Use this evidence to report cyberbullying to web and cell phone service providers.

  • Block the person who is cyberbullying.

Messages are permanently recorded.

Messages can’t be edited or deleted after they’re sent. Users can always access a full history of the messages they’ve sent and received on Involvvely, even if other users delete their accounts

Seek help if you need it.

If you’re a victim of bullying or worried about being a danger to yourself or others, please use any of the following resources:

  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline
  • Stop Bullying: Speak Up
  • iKeepSafe
